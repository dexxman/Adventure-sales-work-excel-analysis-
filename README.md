## 💡 Key Insights

1. **Profit tripled in 3 years** from ₦13.40M (2005) to ₦42.55M (2007), driven by volume growth, not margin improvement (margins stayed ~40–42% throughout)
2. **2007 was the inflection point** transactions jumped from 2,680 to 24,440 (+813%), and qty sold surged from 28k to 256k, signalling a major channel or distribution expansion
3. **Product leadership shifted in 2007**  Mountain-200 (Black/Silver) replaced Road-150 Red, indicating portfolio diversification before reverting in 2008
4. **Sell-through rate is chronically low**  606 products available every year, yet only 25–133 are ever sold; 73–96% of inventory generates zero profit
5. **Expensive products are the business** they consistently drive 84–97% of profit every year; the budget segment is negligible
6. **Weekday bias is consistent** ~72% of profit comes from weekday transactions across all four years
7. **2008 data is incomplete** the July cliff-drop and zero Q3/Q4 profit strongly suggest a data cutoff mid-year, not a real business downturn

## ✅ Recommendations & Solutions

### 🔴 Inventory Rationalisation
**Problem:** Up to 581 of 606 products are unsold in any given year.  
**Recommendation:** Conduct an ABC analysis — classify products into A (top sellers), B (occasional), C (never sold). Discontinue or reduce stock of C-category items to free capital and reduce carrying costs.

### 🟡 Product Portfolio Diversification
**Problem:** Over-reliance on a single product family (Road-150 Red or Mountain-200) creates revenue concentration risk.  
**Recommendation:** Invest in marketing and bundling for mid-tier products. The 2007 shift toward Mountain-200 and broader colour sales (Black, Silver, Yellow) shows demand exists — build on that momentum.

### 🟢 Q3/Q4 Revenue Gap (2005 & 2008)
**Problem:** Q3 and Q4 show near-zero or very low profit in some years.  
**Recommendation:** Introduce seasonal promotions, loyalty programmes, or B2B contracts to smooth quarterly revenue. Identify if the slump is demand-driven or supply/fulfilment-driven.

### 🔵 Less Expensive Products
**Problem:** Budget-tier products contribute only 2.6–19.2% of profit despite likely comprising a larger portion of the catalogue.  
**Recommendation:** Either reposition or bundle less expensive products with premium ones to lift basket value, or investigate why their margins are so thin compared to premium lines.

### 🟣 Geographic Market Expansion
**Problem:** The U.S. accounts for 7,819 customers vs. Canada's 1,571 — a 5x gap despite both being English-speaking Western markets.  
**Recommendation:** Allocate increased marketing spend to Canada, Germany, and France where customer counts are low (1,571–1,810) but market potential likely mirrors the U.S.

### ⚪ Data Completeness (2008)
**Problem:** 2008 data appears to cut off in July, making year-end comparisons misleading.  
**Recommendation:** Ensure the Power BI data pipeline is refreshed with full-year 2008 data. Implement automated data freshness alerts in the dashboard to flag incomplete periods.
